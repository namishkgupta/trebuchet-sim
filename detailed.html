Readout Key

Beam angle θ: Angle of the throwing beam relative to the horizontal.
Sling angle relative to arm: The Angle of Chudness—the unwrapped angle between the sling and long arm.
Projectile speed: Current total speed of the projectile, whether attached or in flight.
Elapsed time: Total simulated time since the run began.
Max speed reached: Highest attached-projectile speed found during the powered swing.
Sling-to-arm angle where speed peaks: Angle of Chudness at the maximum attached-projectile speed.
θ where speed peaks: Beam angle when the maximum attached-projectile speed occurs.
Status: Current state of the simulation, such as idle, running, landed, or error.
Release status: Whether the projectile is attached, released, or landed.
Speed at release: Projectile speed at the exact release moment.
Projectile launch angle: Direction of the projectile velocity at release, measured from the horizontal.
Sling angle to ground: Absolute sling angle relative to the horizontal at release.
Release velocity (vx, vy): Horizontal and vertical projectile velocity components at release.
Release height above ground: Projectile height above ground at release.
Range from pivot base: Horizontal landing position measured from the vertical line beneath the pivot.
Flight time: Time from release until the projectile reaches the ground.
Horizontal travel after release: Horizontal distance from the release point to the landing point.
Impact speed: Projectile speed immediately before it reaches the ground.
Inputs
The sling-to-arm angle starts at 180° (sling folded flat back along the beam) and increases as the sling whips around — watch the live readout and the "chudness at max speed" stat below to pick a sensible target.
L1 (short/CW arm): 0.42318 m
L2 (long/throw arm): 2.01682 m
Pivot height: 1.22 m
θ range: —
Model: coupled Lagrangian dynamics are used for the beam, projectile/sling, and counterweight. Projectile mass contributes to the system inertia and gravitational torque. Beam rotational inertia is calculated from the entered beam mass and the fixed short/long arm geometry. Choose either a fixed counterweight or a freely swinging hinged counterweight with an adjustable hanger length. The sling and hinged counterweight are modeled as massless links with point masses at their ends.

Release is controlled by the selected sling-to-arm angle. The simulator detects the upward crossing and interpolates within the integration step to estimate the exact release state. If the selected angle is never reached, the run stops without an arbitrary fallback release. The peak-speed readout covers only the attached portion of the run, so you can compare your chosen release point against where the theoretical peak actually is.
